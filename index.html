What are and what is the porpuse of the chancelleries?


The Chancelleries, also know as Ministries of Foreign Affairs (MFA) are organs of the State administration that are in charge of preparing, proposing and implementing foreign policy of a determined country. The MFAs are headed by ministers or secretaries, depending on the name they have (in countries like the United States, for example, this entity is called Secretary of State) while in countries like Germany, Chancellery is the name given to the head of the Federal Government, and not to the MFA. Among the multiple functions that MFAs perform we can name: developing and maintaining diplomatic relations between countries; have a Foreign Service distributed in the network of embassies and consulates around the world, as well as as in diplomatic missions to international organizations; perform protocol tasks; and negotiate treaties, agreements and other legal instruments to be adopted by the States.
